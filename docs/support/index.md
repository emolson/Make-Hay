# Make Hay Support

Last updated: April 13, 2026

Make Hay is a self-control app that helps you temporarily limit access to chosen apps until you reach your daily fitness goals.

## Getting Help

If you run into a problem, please open a GitHub issue:

- https://github.com/emolson/Make-Hay/issues

When reporting a problem, include:

- Your iPhone model
- Your iOS version
- The version of Make Hay you are using
- The goal type you configured
- What you expected to happen
- What actually happened

## Common Questions

### Health data is not updating

Try the following:

1. Open Make Hay and go to Settings.
2. Tap Refresh Sync.
3. If tracking still has not started, open the Health app.
4. Tap your profile in the top-right corner.
5. Open Apps & Services.
6. Select Make Hay.
7. Confirm the requested categories are enabled.

### The app cannot block apps

Make Hay needs Screen Time / Family Controls permission in order to apply app restrictions.

If blocking is unavailable:

1. Open Make Hay and go to Settings.
2. Review the Screen Time permission row.
3. Re-request permission if available.
4. If needed, open the app's iOS Settings page and review permission status there.

### I just finished activity, but my apps are still blocked

iOS can delay background health delivery. Open Make Hay and tap Refresh Sync to force an immediate re-check.

### The simulator is not behaving correctly

Apple's Family Controls and Managed Settings behavior is limited on the iOS Simulator. Test app blocking behavior on a physical iPhone.

## What Make Hay Supports

Make Hay currently supports:

- iPhone
- English
- On-device goal evaluation
- Read-only Apple Health integration
- User-selected app blocking through Apple's Screen Time / Family Controls APIs
